Why the rebuild looks completely different

v2 was built on odds-derived projections (R²=0.65 fit). The DG decomposition is a different class of signal — it's a 5-component decomposition of true SG (baseline + age + course history + course fit + distance/accuracy adjustments) trained on 150 measured rounds. The shifts:

Putnam → out (WD). Mouw, Mitchell, and Wyndham Clark also dropped from the top-10 builds — DG sees them at lower SG than v3's odds-derived signal had. The $7-7.8k tier is now dominated by Tom Kim, Doug Ghim, Max McGreevy, and J.L. Smith, who all show DG SG > +0.44 with ΔFit positive (market underrating).

Jordan Spieth elevated to co-stud. DG ranks him #3 in the field (+1.30 SG), course history adjustment of +0.085 is the highest among top-15 players, and odds steamed +2000→+1750. PME and RRG both flagged him as a "ceiling play with high variance" — DG agrees he's a legitimate top-3 talent here. The dual-stud structure is novel from prior versions.

Trade-offs: 79.9% cumulative own is above the audit's 60% target (Bug 15). The Spieth + Si Woo combo will be popular post-steam, but it's still better than a Scheffler-anchored build (-25 projected pts). The contrarian alt swaps in Bezuidenhout to bring own down to ~73%.
