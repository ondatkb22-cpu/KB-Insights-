The course is not the course you remember.

TPC Craig Ranch underwent a $25 million Lanny Wadkins renovation over seven months in 2025. Greens were rebuilt with 777 Bentgrass (faster, smoother), fairways converted to Stadium Zoysia, rough to TifTuf Bermuda. All 18 greens were reshaped — smaller and more undulated — and the entire bunker complex was repositioned. The 18th was lengthened and the green moved alongside Rowlett Creek to turn an "easy birdie" into a demanding par 4. The par-3 4th now has a Biarritz green with a trench down the middle.

What this means for the model: past results at Craig Ranch (2021–2025) are partial information at best. The 26-under historical winning average is the prior, not the projection. Course history weight gets cut in half. Approach proximity to small greens replaces "putter is king" as the dominant skill.

The week after the major is a strange one. The signature events are over, the Texas swing is winding down, and most of the world's top 20 are home resting. Of the players who carried our Aronimink card last week — Scheffler, McIlroy, C. Young, M. Fitzpatrick, Schauffele, Aberg, Rose — only Scheffler is committed to McKinney. The rest are out.

That makes this a second-tier-but-deep field with a wide-open without-Scheffler market. The 2025 edition had only seven top-50 OWGR players. Expect roughly the same in 2026. Jordan Spieth, Sungjae Im, Sam Burns, defending champ Taylor Pendrith, Stephan Jaeger, Si Woo Kim, Tom Kim, and Ben An anchor the second tier.

The bigger story is the course. The Wadkins renovation is the central unknown, and it's the kind of unknown that breaks models. The pre-reno course was an easy birdiefest where Scheffler shot 31-under last year and the putter was king. The new course has smaller greens, repositioned bunkers, and a redesigned closing hole — a setup tuned to punish sloppy approach play that the old layout shrugged off.

So the lessons from the Aronimink lineup translate, with one twist: form and approach still rule, but course history must be discounted, and we have to wait for the first 18 holes Thursday before trusting any of last year's profiles.

The shape of victory.

Two long spokes this week: approach and putting. Distance is moderate (course is plenty long but the pre-reno fairways were so wide that big hitters had no edge — that may tighten now). Accuracy is the lowest spoke on tour. This is the anti-Aronimink.

01
Approach to smaller new greens. The single biggest reno change. SG:APP from 125–175y is the highest-leverage skill of the week. Iron specialists win.
02
Putting still matters, but on new bentgrass. Players who putt well on bent in general (Spieth, Burns, McCarthy) get a one-week edge over Bermuda specialists.
03
Birdie-or-better rate is the floor stat. Winning scores at Craig Ranch since 2021: -26, -26, -23, -23, -31. You need a player who can string birdies. Pure pars don't cash.
04
Course history is half-weight. Old Craig Ranch is gone. Use 2021–2025 finishes only as tiebreakers, not primary signal.

What Carries Over From Aronimink

5 calibrations to bake into Phase 2
  1. Hard-filter to form_tag ∈ {LOCKED, TRENDING, HOT} before the optimizer runs. Every player in the lineup that survived R1 at Aronimink was form-tagged. Don't let NEUTRAL or FADE players in by value alone.
  2. Promote vs_baseline_l20 from secondary to primary signal. Alex Fitzpatrick (+2.02 vs BL) and Andrew Putnam (+0.83) were the value plays. The signal "player is exceeding his own track record" beat raw SG L20 alone.
  3. Keep the Stars+Scrubs structure for GPP — but rebalance for Craig Ranch. At a -25 birdiefest, ceiling matters more than floor. Drop the 2/2 stud requirement at $9k+ and allow 1 stud + 5 mid/value as a viable structure when the winning score is -25.
  4. Maintain the $49,500–$49,900 salary corridor. Don't widen it just because the value tier at Byron Nelson is deeper. The corridor enforces the leverage discipline.
  5. Cut course-history weight in half. Aronimink-history was rare and signal-rich (Bradley won the '18 BMW). Craig Ranch-history points at a different golf course. Pre-reno results are tiebreakers, not anchors.
